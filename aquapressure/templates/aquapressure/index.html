The Cognitive Resilience Project

Our Mission

Our brain functions are highly vulnerable to the effects and stress and these stress-induced cognitive impairments may contribute to our learning or our mental wellness. But some individuals are more effective than others in maintaining their cognitive abilities in the face of stress, demonstrating cognitive resilience. Studying how our brain functions varying in the context of stress and how individuals vary in cognitive resilience is critical in exploring factors that preserve our well-being.

How do we measure these core brain functions under stress? The Cognitive Resilience Project set out to do exactly that: develop a tool to capture changes in our core cognitive functions under stress and implement a series of experimental studies to investigate these relations.

Our Concepts

This challenge can be viewed in a framework that has been informed by a large body of psychological and neuroscience research, namely that of executive functions – core cognitive skills that are highly predictive of academic and professional life outcomes. Effective executive function is considered to be essential for daily life but is particularly vulnerable when individuals are exposed to stress. Our work is the first to assess executive functions in the context of stress. Moreover, this work may serve as the first game-based behavioral measure of emotion regulation—the processes by which an individual influences which feelings they have, when they have them, and how they experience feelings.

Our Game: AquaPressure

A vast number of executive function assessments currently exist in both academia and for use in the general public. However, the assessments currently available are undermined by a number of validity issues and more importantly, do not assess executive functions in the context of stress. AquaPressure was developed to do just that: assess a player’s executive function skills as they navigate an underwater maze in varying stressful conditions!

Our Research

After creating this research tool for collecting data on executive functions in a socio-emotional context, we are in the midst of conducting a series of research studies to investigate executive functions and stress.

Our Student Dashboard

For educators interested in bringing executive function to the classroom, we have developed a modified version of Aquapressure in addition to a series of supporting activities that will engage students while testing their executive functions! Our classroom version of Aquapressure has a dashboard to show players their performance across their different executive function skills, and our activity series offers a series of playful activities designed to help students understand the role of their executive functions in their everyday life.

Our Team

We are cognitive neuroscientists, game designers, programmers, and educators working together to harness the science of cognitive resilience to improve human learning and mental health.
